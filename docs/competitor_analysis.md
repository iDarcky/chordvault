# Competitor & Acquisition Analysis

Setlists MD brings a modern, lightweight, cross-platform (React 19 / Capacitor), and privacy-first (Markdown/Local-First) approach to the worship and gigging musician space.

This document outlines the competitive landscape and potential acquisition targets.

## 1. Potential Acquirers & The Strategic Fit (The "Why")

*   **MultiTracks (ChartBuilder):** A massive ecosystem heavily tied to their expensive backing tracks. They might acquire Setlists MD to offer a "lite," web-first, cross-platform alternative to attract smaller churches or solo musicians who can't afford their main platform, or to strip the Markdown parsing/rendering engine to upgrade their own apps.
*   **WorshipTools (Music Stand):** They offer a suite of church software. If Setlists MD outpaces them in modern UI/UX, responsiveness, or offline-first sync architecture (PWA/Capacitor), they might acquire to replace their aging codebase.
*   **OnSong:** The giant of iPad chord rendering. OnSong is infamously complex and bloated. If they ever decide they need a sleek, modern, cross-platform web/Android presence, acquiring a React-based app like Setlists MD would save them years of dev time.
*   **Muse Group (Ultimate Guitar / MuseScore):** They are aggressively acquiring tools for gigging musicians. If Setlists MD proves to be the best *markdown/text-based* chart renderer, it fits perfectly into their portfolio alongside their tab and sheet music properties.
*   **Planning Center (PCO):** Highly likely to do an **acqui-hire**. If the rendering engine and integrations are top-tier, they might buy the app just to acquire the engineering talent and sunset Setlists MD.

## 2. The Competitor Landscape

**The Worship Space (Enterprise/Ecosystem heavy):**
1.  **Planning Center (Music Stand):** The 800lb gorilla. PDF-heavy, requires the whole PCO ecosystem.
2.  **MultiTracks (ChartBuilder):** Deeply integrated with their backing tracks and paid charts.
3.  **WorshipTools (Music Stand):** Good cross-platform presence, but UI can feel dated.
4.  **PraiseCharts:** More of a content distributor, but they dabble in apps.

**The Broad Gigging / Musician Space (Feature-heavy, often complex):**
1.  **OnSong:** The industry standard for iOS. Huge feature set, but very expensive, steep learning curve, and historically weak outside of the Apple ecosystem.
2.  **forScore:** Pure PDF management (iOS only). Beautiful, but lacks dynamic text/transposition.
3.  **SongbookPro:** Very similar market to Setlists MD. Cross-platform, handles text-based formats (ChordPro). A direct competitor for the solo gigging musician.
4.  **BandHelper / Setlist Maker:** Extremely powerful but has a famously clunky, dated UI.
5.  **Ultimate Guitar Pro:** Focuses more on tabs and discovery.

## 3. Acquisition Valuation Scenarios

**Scenario A: The "Tech Buy" or Acqui-hire ($50k – $250k)**
*   **State:** Beautiful, working app, polished codebase, low MRR.
*   **Buyer:** OnSong or WorshipTools wanting the React/Tailwind/Markdown rendering engine or lead engineer.

**Scenario B: Early Traction & Niche Player ($300k – $1M)**
*   **State:** Loyal user base, solid monetization tier, generating ~$5k - $15k MRR.
*   **Buyer:** Muse Group or mid-size tech private equity.
*   **Valuation:** 3x to 5x Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR).

**Scenario C: Proven MRR & Strategic Threat ($1M – $5M+)**
*   **State:** $30k+ MRR, actively pulling churches away from Planning Center or MultiTracks.
*   **Buyer:** MultiTracks or Planning Center.
*   **Valuation:** Strategic acquisitions command premiums of 5x to 10x ARR.
