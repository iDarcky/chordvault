# Setlists MD vs. Planning Center (PCO)

If your church already uses Planning Center Services, you might be wondering why you would want a separate app for your chord charts. Planning Center is the industry standard for service scheduling and volunteer management. However, when it comes to the *actual performance*—the moments on stage—Setlists MD offers a profoundly different and often superior approach.

Here is why teams are choosing Setlists MD for their music stand, even if they use PCO for scheduling.

## 1. Data Ownership vs. The Walled Garden
* **Planning Center:** Your chord charts live inside PCO's database. If you decide to cancel your subscription, or if PCO changes their pricing model, you lose seamless access to your formatted charts. Exporting them often means downloading hundreds of static PDFs.
* **Setlists MD:** Your songs are `.md` (Markdown) files. You own them. They live on your device or in your own Google Drive. Even if Setlists MD ceases to exist, your files are perfectly readable plain text. You are never locked in.

## 2. Dynamic Rendering vs. Static Files
* **Planning Center:** PCO Music Stand relies heavily on PDFs. If the worship leader decides to change the key during rehearsal, someone has to log into PCO, transpose the chart, generate a new PDF, and everyone has to refresh their app to get the new file.
* **Setlists MD:** Charts are rendered dynamically on the device. Changing the key, toggling Nashville Numbers, or adding a capo happens instantly with a single tap, without needing to communicate with a server.

## 3. Offline Reliability vs. Cloud Dependency
* **Planning Center:** While the Music Stand app caches files, PCO is fundamentally a cloud-based service. If the internet goes down at your church, accessing new files, transposing, or handling last-minute setlist changes becomes incredibly difficult.
* **Setlists MD:** It is an "offline-first" application. Your entire library and the rendering engine live locally on your device. You have 100% functionality whether you have gigabit Wi-Fi or zero signal.

## 4. Frictionless Access vs. Account Management
* **Planning Center:** To view a chart, a musician needs a PCO account, needs to be scheduled for that specific service, and needs the correct permissions to view the files. Managing this for guest musicians or substitute players is a headache.
* **Setlists MD:** Zero friction. No accounts required. Want to share a setlist with a guest bassist? Just send them a zip file or a link. They open the app and the music is there.

## 5. Built for the Musician, Not the Administrator
* **Planning Center:** PCO is built top-down for administrators, planners, and worship pastors. The Music Stand app is an extension of that administrative system.
* **Setlists MD:** Built ground-up for the musician on stage. Features like the built-in Capo Calculator, support for mid-song Modulations (key changes), and the ability to add your own personal inline notes without affecting the master chart make it a better tool for live performance.

## Summary

| Feature | Setlists MD | Planning Center |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Focus** | Performance & Rendering | Administration & Scheduling |
| **Data Format** | Open Markdown (`.md`) | Proprietary Database / PDF |
| **Data Ownership** | 100% Yours | Locked in PCO |
| **Offline Capability** | Full | Partial (Cached viewing only) |
| **Instant Transpose** | Yes (Rendered instantly) | Yes (Requires network/re-generation) |
| **Accounts Required** | None | Required for all users |
| **Cost** | Free | Subscription based |

**The Verdict:** Continue using Planning Center to schedule your team and plan your service flow. But when the band steps onto the stage, switch to Setlists MD for a faster, more reliable, and musician-focused experience.
