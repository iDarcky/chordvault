# Setlists MD vs. Planning Center (Music Stand)

Planning Center Online (PCO) is the industry standard for large churches. However, it is built for **administrators**, not necessarily **musicians**. This is where Setlists MD wins.

---

## 1. Why Choose Setlists MD instead of PCO?

### A. Data Ownership vs. Data Lockdown
*   **PCO:** Your songs are locked in their proprietary database. If you stop paying, you lose access to your formatting and charts.
*   **Setlists MD:** Your songs are `.md` files in your own Google Drive. You own them forever. Setlists MD is just a "lens" to look at them.

### B. The "Static PDF" Problem
*   **PCO:** Most churches upload PDFs to PCO. Transposing a PDF is impossible without a new file.
*   **Setlists MD:** Everything is **dynamic text**. You can transpose, toggle Nashville Numbers, and change the layout (1-col vs. 2-col) instantly on stage.

### C. Offline Reliability
*   **PCO Music Stand:** Can be "glitchy" when the church Wi-Fi drops (common in older buildings).
*   **Setlists MD:** Built as an **Offline-First PWA**. It is designed to work in a basement with zero bars of signal.

### D. Mid-Song Modulation
*   **PCO:** Does not natively support mid-song key changes in a single chart.
*   **Setlists MD:** Has first-class support for `{modulate: +2}` markers that update the chord diagrams and transpositions automatically as you scroll.

### E. Price & Barrier to Entry
*   **PCO:** Requires a monthly subscription to "Services" and often "Music Stand."
*   **Setlists MD:** Core features are **Free**. A guest musician can scan a QR code and see the chart without even creating an account.

## 2. Marketing Strategy: How to Market Against Them

### Message 1: "The Small Church Hero"
*   **Angle:** PCO is "overkill" for a 3-person worship team.
*   **Hook:** "Planning Center is for the office. Setlists MD is for the stage."
*   **Target:** Small-to-medium churches that find PCO too expensive or complex.

### Message 2: "Musicians First, Admins Second"
*   **Angle:** PCO is built for scheduling volunteers. Setlists MD is built for playing music.
*   **Hook:** "Charts that move with you. Transpose, modulate, and switch to Nashville Numbers with one tap."
*   **Target:** Worship leaders who are tired of managing PDF attachments.

### Message 3: "Own Your Ministry's Assets"
*   **Angle:** Don't let your song library be a hostage to a subscription.
*   **Hook:** "Your songs are your files. Hosted in your Drive. Accessible forever."
*   **Target:** Tech-savvy worship leaders who value data portability.

### Message 4: "Zero-Friction Guest Onboarding"
*   **Angle:** Guest bassists shouldn't have to download an app and reset their PCO password just to play one Sunday.
*   **Hook:** "Scan. Play. Done. No accounts for guests."
*   **Target:** Teams that frequently use guest musicians or have high volunteer turnover.

## 3. Comparison Table

| Feature | Planning Center (Music Stand) | Setlists MD |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Format** | Mostly Static PDFs | Dynamic Markdown (.md) |
| **Data Ownership** | Locked in PCO | Your Google Drive / Dropbox |
| **Offline** | Requires prior sync | Offline-First by design |
| **Guest Access** | Requires Login/Invite | Scan QR (No account) |
| **Modulation** | Manual / Multi-file | Native `{modulate}` markers |
| **Nashville #s** | Rare / Hard to setup | One-tap toggle |
| **Price** | Paid Subscription | **Free** (Paid Private Sync) |

---

## 4. Competitive "Trojan Horse" Feature
To win, Setlists MD should implement a **"PCO Import"** tool.
*   "Connect your Planning Center account, and we'll pull your lyrics and keys into `.md` files for you."
*   Once their data is in your format, they are much more likely to switch.
